# Profile Setup

Your **profile** is how you appear to colleagues in Next.js SaaS AI Template: in People Finder, on projects, in 1:1s, and in the directory. This guide explains how to edit your profile and set preferences so the platform works well for you.

---

## Where to Edit Your Profile

- From **My View**, open **Profile** (or **Profile settings**) from the sidebar, or use the **account/avatar menu** in the header and choose “Profile” or “Settings”.
- You may also land on a profile page from a “Complete your profile” prompt on first login.

The profile form is usually grouped into sections: **Basic info**, **Links** (GitHub, LinkedIn), **Preferences** (timezone, locale), and sometimes **Privacy**.

---

## Basic Information

These fields define who you are in the tenant.

| Field      | What it’s for                                                                                                                |
| ---------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Name**   | Your full name or display name. Shown in search, directory, and org chart.                                                   |
| **Title**  | Job title or role (e.g. “Software Engineer”, “Product Manager”). Helps others understand your role.                          |
| **Bio**    | Short summary about you, your focus areas, or how you like to work. Optional but useful for People Finder and introductions. |
| **Avatar** | Profile picture. Improves recognition in lists, 1:1s, and feedback.                                                          |

### Steps to update basic info

1. Open **Profile** (or **Profile settings**).
2. Edit **Name** and **Title** as needed.
3. Add or change your **Bio** in the text area.
4. For **Avatar**: use the upload or “Change photo” control; follow the instructions for supported formats and size.
5. Save (e.g. **Save** or **Update profile**). Changes are reflected across the app.

> **Tip:** A clear, up-to-date title and a short bio make it easier for colleagues to find you in People Finder and understand how you can help.

## Connect GitHub and LinkedIn

Linking external accounts helps Next.js SaaS AI Template (and optionally AI) understand your skills and professional presence.

- **GitHub** — Connects your GitHub profile. Can be used for evidence, skills inference, or portfolio links (depending on tenant configuration).
- **LinkedIn** — Connects your LinkedIn profile. Often used for professional identity and optional skills/experience signals.

### How to connect

1. In your profile, find the **Links** or **Connected accounts** section.
2. Click **Connect** (or similar) next to **GitHub** or **LinkedIn**.
3. Complete the OAuth flow in the popup or redirect (authorize Next.js SaaS AI Template to access the requested data).
4. After authorization, the account appears as “Connected” and you may see a **Disconnect** option.

Disconnecting removes the link and any data pulled from that account; your profile and skills are not deleted, but evidence or inferences that relied on that connection may be affected depending on tenant rules.

## Timezone and Location

Setting **timezone** (and optionally **location**) helps with:

- Scheduling 1:1s and meetings.
- Showing times in your local zone in the app.
- Filters or analytics that use location (if your tenant uses them).

### Steps

1. In profile or **Preferences**, find **Timezone**.
2. Select your timezone from the dropdown (e.g. “Europe/Madrid”, “America/New_York”).
3. If **Location** (city, country, or region) is available, fill it in as needed.
4. Save. The app will use this for date/time display and scheduling where applicable.

> **Tip:** Use a city or region that matches your actual work location so meeting times and reminders are correct.

---

## Personal Email and Pronouns

- **Personal email** — If the profile has a “Personal email” or “Contact email” field (separate from the login email), you can set it for contact or recovery. What is shown to others depends on tenant privacy settings.
- **Pronouns** — If your tenant supports **pronouns** (e.g. “she/her”, “he/him”, “they/them”), you can select or type them in the profile. They are typically shown next to your name in the directory, 1:1s, and feedback to foster inclusive communication.

Edit these in the same profile form, usually under “Contact” or “Identity”, and save.

---

## After You Save

- Your **name**, **title**, **avatar**, and **pronouns** (if set) appear in the directory, org chart, People Finder, and when you give or receive feedback.
- **GitHub/LinkedIn** links may be used for evidence and skills (depending on configuration).
- **Timezone** and **location** are used for scheduling and display.
- Revisit your profile whenever your role, focus, or preferences change so the platform stays accurate and useful for you and your team.

For more on moving around the app, see [Navigation & Views](/docs/en/getting-started/navigation). For member-specific features (skills, OKRs, learning), use the **Member** section of the docs.
